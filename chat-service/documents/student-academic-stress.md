# Managing Academic Stress

Specialized guidance for students managing the pressures of academic life.

## Exam Stress Management
- Create a realistic study schedule well before exams
- Take regular breaks using the Pomodoro technique (25 minutes focused work, 5 minute break)
- Practice deep breathing before and during exams
- Get adequate sleep during exam periods — pulling all-nighters usually hurts performance more than it helps
- Break large amounts of material into smaller, manageable chunks
- Avoid comparing your preparation to others

## Study-Life Balance
- Schedule time for relaxation and social activities, not just study
- Set clear boundaries between study time and personal time
- Prioritize self-care during busy periods, not just after them
- Learn to say no to unnecessary commitments
- Academic pressure is real, but it should not consume every part of life

## Focus Enhancement
- Minimize distractions during study sessions (phone in another room, notifications off)
- Use the Pomodoro technique for focused work blocks
- Practice mindfulness to improve attention span over time
- Create a dedicated study space separate from where you relax or sleep

## When Academic Pressure Becomes Something More
Persistent academic stress that includes hopelessness, loss of interest in things once enjoyed, major changes in sleep or appetite, or thoughts of self-harm is no longer "just stress" — it may indicate depression or anxiety that benefits from professional support. Encourage using MindEase's PHQ-9/GAD-7 screening tools and booking a session with a campus counsellor.
