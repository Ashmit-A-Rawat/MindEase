# Understanding MindEase's Screening Tools

MindEase offers three standardized, validated mental health screening questionnaires. These are self-report tools, not diagnostic instruments — a high score means "this warrants professional attention," not "you have been diagnosed."

## PHQ-9 (Patient Health Questionnaire-9)
Screens for depression severity based on 9 questions covering the past two weeks, including mood, interest in activities, sleep, energy, appetite, concentration, and thoughts of self-harm.
- 0-4: Minimal symptoms
- 5-9: Mild symptoms
- 10-14: Moderate symptoms
- 15-19: Moderately severe symptoms
- 20-27: Severe symptoms

Question 9 specifically asks about thoughts of self-harm or death — any non-zero answer here is treated as a safety-critical signal within MindEase, regardless of the total score.

## GAD-7 (Generalized Anxiety Disorder-7)
Screens for anxiety severity based on 7 questions about worry, restlessness, irritability, and related symptoms over the past two weeks.
- 0-4: Minimal anxiety
- 5-9: Mild anxiety
- 10-14: Moderate anxiety
- 15-21: Severe anxiety

## GHQ-12 (General Health Questionnaire-12)
A broader screen for general psychological wellbeing and distress, covering concentration, sleep, decision-making, and overall coping, useful as a general check rather than a specific-condition screen.

## What To Do With a Result
Regardless of score, these tools are a starting point for a conversation, not a verdict. Encourage the student to review their results in MindEase's Reports section and consider booking a session with a campus counsellor, especially for Moderate or higher scores. These tools should never replace an actual conversation with a mental health professional.
